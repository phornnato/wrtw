Historical and Mystical Marvel

The Legacy, Architecture, and Global Appeal of the Sacred Temple

Founded by Jayavarman VII in the late 12th century, Ta Prohm was originally named Rajavihara (“Royal Monastery”). It served as a significant Buddhist center, fostering scholarly and religious activities.

Ta Prohm’s layout includes a central sanctuary within rectangular enclosures, highlighted by iconic face towers and bas-reliefs. Its unique integration with nature, including the dramatic growth of silk-cotton and strangler fig trees, adds to its mystical charm.

He dedicated Ta Prohm to his mother, and the temple once had 80,000 people, including 18 high priests and 615 female dancers, involved in its service.

Featured in "Lara Croft: Tomb Raider" (2001), Ta Prohm’s portrayal as a lost, enigmatic site has dramatically increased its global popularity and tourist interest.
